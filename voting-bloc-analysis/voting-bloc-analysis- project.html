Abstract¶

This assessment utilizes Market Basket Analysis (MBA) and Association Rule Mining on the 2022 Philippine Senatorial Elections dataset. By conceptualizing provinces as transactions and the top eight senatorial candidates as items, the research applies the Apriori algorithm to identify recurring voting patterns and political blocs. The analysis further quantifies senator-to-senator relationships using association rules, visualized in a lift heatmap. The results provide a data-driven perspective on coalition behavior and regional voting blocks, offering insights into the nature of candidate support across the Philippines.

Name one limitation of using province-level aggregation for this MBA analysis. How might results differ if you used municipality-level transactions instead?¶

The main limitation is aggregating votes to the province level, which disregards voting differences within individuals or smaller groups in that area. By treating an entire province as a single transaction, the analysis assumes a smooth transition of the unique political leanings of different cities or rural districts.

If municipality-level transactions were used instead, the analysis could reveal more detailed, localized voting alliances, potentially uncovering stronger or more distinct association patterns among candidates.
